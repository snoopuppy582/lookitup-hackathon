### Source Pack

`Breaking News + Fact-Check Pack`

## F. Demo Dataset

### Demo 1 - Kamchatka AI Snow

Claim:

> "Kamchatka snow reached the height of a 10-story apartment building."

Expected status:

> Trusted sources say: AI-generated visuals spread online. Claim says: 10-story snow was real.

Why this works:

- It is a real cross-border verification failure.
- It shows why source evidence must arrive before publication.
- It carries newsroom stakes in one visible claim.

### Demo 2 - Macron Title Mismatch

Claim:

> "Emmanuel Macron is the Vice President of France."

Expected status:

> Trusted sources say: President. Claim says: Vice President.

Why this works:

- It is simple enough for backup.
- It mirrors the panel's inconsistency example.

Why this works:

- It shows missing evidence.
- It reinforces source-boundary control.

## K. Q&A Defense

| Question | Answer |
| --- | --- |
| Why not Google? | Google searches the whole web. Lookitup searches only inside sources the journalist chose before the deadline. |
| Why not ChatGPT? | ChatGPT gives generated text. Lookitup shows source-bound evidence. |
| Is this automatic fact-checking? | It is pre-publication evidence search. The journalist decides. |
| Why start with local data? | The pitch runs from local data. URL/RSS ingestion comes after the core loop works. |
| Is Kamchatka an image-verification product? | Kamchatka is the problem case. The P0 product is trusted-source evidence search before publication. |
| What is the technical core? | Controlled retrieval: source packs define the search boundary, and Evidence Cards make evidence visible. |
