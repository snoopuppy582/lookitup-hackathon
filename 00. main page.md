# Lookitup

## One-Liner

Lookitup stops unverified claims before they become published news.

## Core Claim

Publication needs visible evidence before a doubtful claim goes live.

## Why This Exists

A fake AI snow visual from Russia's Kamchatka Peninsula moved across social platforms and was reported by Korean media as real. The claim was simple and visual: snow had reached the height of a 10-story apartment building.

Fact-checkers later showed the evidence trail. The visuals were AI-generated, actual snow depth was far lower than the viral claim implied, and some Korean broadcasts deleted related videos after publication.

That is the newsroom failure Lookitup targets:

> A claim reached publication before the evidence caught up.

## Panel Fit

The journalist panel described the same pressure in newsroom language:

- "maximum five minutes" for breaking-news alerts
- "15 minutes" for an article
- "100% correct"
- "clients absolutely trust us 100%"
- "I never use AI ... I do not trust it" for verification
- a useful tool would highlight "inconsistencies"

## Product

Lookitup is a pre-publication evidence workspace for journalists.

The core loop is short:

> select trusted sources -> search a claim -> receive Evidence Cards -> decide before publishing

## Live Prototype Scope

Build only the core loop:

- preset Trusted Source Packs
- claim or subject search
- local trusted-source corpus
- Evidence Cards
- source links
- not-found state
