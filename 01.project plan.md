# 1. Project Plan

## Main Claim

**LookItUp creates a source boundary before publication.**

## Tagline

**LookItUp: Turn doubt into evidence.**

## One-Liner

LookItUp stops unverified claims before they become published news.

## Main Question

How can a journalist check one doubtful claim against trusted sources before publishing?

## Problem

Newsrooms now receive claims faster than source evidence.

The Kamchatka AI snow case shows the failure. AI-generated visuals from Russia spread online. Korean media reported scenes that looked like snow reaching the height of a 10-story apartment building. Fact-checkers later showed that the viral visuals were AI-generated and that the physical claim lacked source support.

The failure was publication before evidence.

## Panel Evidence

The journalist panel gave the newsroom version of the same problem.

Helena Pilar Gisbert from AFP described breaking-news alerts moving in "maximum five minutes" and articles moving in "15 minutes." She also said clients "absolutely trust us 100%" and expect agency copy to be correct.

Gisbert gave the product direction: she checks official sources, does not trust AI for verification, and wants a tool that highlights "inconsistencies."

## Global Source Gap

The Kamchatka case is one example of a wider source-control problem.

> Russia social media -> Korean newsroom -> public report -> global fact-check correction

Reuters Institute 2026 reports that AI chatbot news use rose from 7% to 10% globally, with 16% use among under-35s. EBU/BBC found that 31% of AI assistant news answers had serious sourcing problems.

News moves through platforms and AI. Publication still needs source-level evidence.

## Target User

Primary user:

- a journalist or editor checking a claim before publication

Included workflows:

- news agency translation and editing
- fact-checking desk review
- web publishing before a post goes live
- independent journalism with user-defined trusted sources

## Product Boundary

LookItUp starts with one working loop:

> select a Trusted Source Pack -> search a claim -> read Evidence Cards -> decide before publishing

The source pack defines the boundary. The Evidence Cards make that boundary visible.

## Trusted Source Pack

A Trusted Source Pack is a selected set of sources prepared before the deadline.

Example packs:

- Breaking News + Fact-Check Pack
- International Wire Pack
- France Official Sources Pack
- Local Government and Police Pack
- NGO and Human Rights Pack

## Evidence Cards

Each Evidence Card shows:

- source name
- source type
- matched quote
- date or timestamp
- URL
- ranking score

The journalist decides from visible evidence.

Primary KPI:

Manual workflow:

> claim -> open tabs -> search sources -> judge evidence

LookItUp workflow:

> source pack -> claim search -> Evidence Cards

## Product Principle
