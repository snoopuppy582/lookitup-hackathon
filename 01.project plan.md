## Hook

Google searches the whole web.
ChatGPT gives an answer.
Lookitup shows evidence from sources the journalist chose to trust.

## Problem

Newsrooms now face cross-border claims before they have cross-border evidence.

The Kamchatka AI snow case shows the failure clearly. AI-generated visuals from Russia spread through social platforms. Korean media reported scenes that looked like snow reaching a 10-story apartment building. Fact-checkers later traced the visuals to AI-generated content and showed that the physical claim did not match available evidence.

The problem is publication before evidence.

## Panel Evidence

The panel gave the newsroom version of that problem through named sources:

- Helena Pilar Gisbert, AFP
- Juliette POMMIER, ARTE
- Camille Montagu, RSF

Helena Pilar Gisbert from AFP described breaking-news alerts that must move in "maximum five minutes" and articles that may need to move in "15 minutes." Gisbert said agency clients "absolutely trust us 100%" and expect the copy to be truthful.

Gisbert also gave the product direction: she does not trust AI for verification, checks official sources, and wants a tool that highlights "inconsistencies." That is Lookitup's core loop.

## Why This Is Global

The Kamchatka case is a cross-border verification failure:

> Russia social media -> Korean newsroom -> public report -> global fact-check correction

This pattern is growing. Reuters Institute 2026 reports that AI chatbot news use rose from 7% to 10% globally, with 16% use among under-35s. EBU/BBC found that 45% of AI assistant news answers had at least one significant issue, including 31% with serious sourcing problems and 20% with major accuracy issues.

News distribution is faster than source control. Journalists still carry publication responsibility.

## Product Vision

Lookitup starts as trusted-source search and grows into a newsroom evidence workspace.

The prototype proves one loop:

> select trusted sources -> search a claim or subject -> receive Evidence Cards

The product vision adds newsroom workflow:

- shared source packs by desk, beat, and country
- claim-level evidence attached to drafts
- cited AI summaries from retrieved Evidence Cards
- editor review before publication
- exportable evidence packs
- image, video, and provenance workflows

## Source Pack Model

Lookitup uses preset Trusted Source Packs.

A newsroom, desk editor, fact-checking team, or independent journalist prepares the trusted sources before the deadline. During breaking news, the journalist selects a pack and searches inside that boundary.

Example packs:

- Breaking News + Fact-Check Pack
- International Wire Pack
- France Official Sources Pack
- Local Government and Police Pack
- NGO and Human Rights Pack

## Solution

The user selects a Trusted Source Pack, enters a doubtful claim, and receives Evidence Cards from that source boundary.

Each Evidence Card shows:

- source name
- source type
- matched quote
- date or timestamp
- URL
- ranking score

The journalist makes the publication decision from visible evidence.

Claim:

> Breaking News + Fact-Check Pack

Expected status:

Evidence Cards:

- OhmyNews fact-check: Korean media reported AI snow visuals as real.
- AFP Fact Check: massive Kamchatka snow footage was AI-generated.
- Local sample: actual evidence showed severe snow without support for the 10-story visual claim.
